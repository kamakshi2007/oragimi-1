1.Camel

Camels can reach 7 feet in height (at the hump) and weigh up to 1500 pounds.

They are specially adapted to the life in desert. Their eyes have three eyelids and two rows of eyelashes that prevent sand to enter their eyes.

Camel's ears are furry.

2.Chameleon

Their feet work like salad tongs.

Chameleons vary widely in terms of sizes.

They change their colour according to their will

3.Pigeon

Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.

Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities

Pigeons are highly sociable animals.

4.Teddy bare

Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.

The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.

5.Panda

Pandas have so many fans because they look cute.

Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day

An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!.

6.Flying-cicada

The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.

Females may be attracted to the sound of motors.

Most have red-orange eyes
